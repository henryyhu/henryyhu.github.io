About me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Information and Decision Sciences at College of Business Administration at University of Illinois at Chicago. Prior to joining UIC in Fall 2015, I was a Staff Member in the USER (User Systems and Experience Research) group at IBM Research - Almaden. I obtained my Ph.D in Computer Science at Arizona State University (ASU), where I was an irresponsible student under the great Dr. Subbarao Kambhampati. My dissertation committee also included Dr. Eric Horvitz, Dr. John Krumm, Dr. Huan Liu, and Dr. Hari Sundaram. In the past, I have interned at Microsoft Research and Avaya Labs.

My research interests are in the area of Machine Learning, Social Computing and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Specifically, my interdisciplinary work focuses on analyzing and predicting large-scale, time- varying, heterogeneous human behaviors manifested in their online footprints at both individual and collective levels. My current and past projects include modeling and predicting personality from a person’s social media posts, characterizing the effect of social media sharing for crowdfunding campaigns, and understanding and predicting people’s behaviors, sentiments and engagement with public events and life events.

My research has been awarded a Best Paper Nomination at CHI-13, led to a working system Whoo.ly (http://whooly.net) used by thousands of people, and filed two patents (both granted). Recently, my research attracted attention from Michael Crow -- the president of ASU, and I was honored to receive a letter of commendation from him.

My work has been also featured several times in the media, including WIRED, ABC, PBS, The Seattle Times, FastCompany, Computer Magazine, ASU news, and Neowin.
